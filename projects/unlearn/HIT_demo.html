In this HIT you will be presented with a excerpt from a Wikipedia article that acts as a prompt and a system's automatically-generated continuation of that excerpt. Your job is to rate the quality of the system generation across three axes:

  • Coherence: Is the system's generation aligned in meaning/topic with the prompt?
  • Fluency: Is the system's generation grammatical and easy-to-read?
  • Informativeness: Is the system's generation interesting, plausible, and not simply repeating the prompt?

You will be able to rate each of the three axes on a scale from 1 to 5, with 1 being the lowest/worst and 5 the highest/best. The specific scales are:

  • Coherence:
    • 5 (excellent): The system's result is perfectly in line with the semantic meaning defined by the prompt; topically speaking, I could see this type of completion appearing in a Wikipedia article after the prompt.
    • 4: The system's result is very related to the prompt but with some minor errors that do not affect overall relevance with respect to the prompt.
    • 3: The system's result is, to some extent, relevant to the prompt, but some errors/irrelevant parts display minor semantic inconsistency or contradiction.
    • 2: At the first glance, the system's result seems somewhat related to the prompt, but the semantic inconsistency can be easily spotted.
    • 1 (bad): The system's result is completely off topic, or is semantically contradictory to the content contained in the prompt.
  • Fluency:
    • 5 (excellent): The system's result is human-like, grammatically correct, and very easy to understand. This reads as if it were written by a human.
    • 4: Very good fluency, but I could probably tell a machine wrote it based on a very minor grammar error or incoherent part.
    • 3: The system's result contains minor errors but they do not affect your understanding.
    • 2: Mediocre fluency: grammar/language errors are difficult to overlook, but I managed to understand what it was going for.
    • 1 (bad): The system's result does not make sense and it is unreadable.
  • Informativeness:
    • 5 (excellent): The system's result is very informative, contains novel content, and seems plausible. In addition, it displays the right level of diversity and it is enjoyable to read.
    • 4: The system's output mostly goes beyond the information in the prompt, and it is definitely possible to learn something from it.
    • 3: The system's result contains some new information and it displays a certain level of diversity, but it's either too reptitive/detailed or presents at least one dubious fact.
    • 2: Newly presented information is largely copied from the prompt, and/or there's significant clearly incorrect information presented.
    • 1 (bad): The system's result is dull, repetitive, and does not have new information. All its content has already been provided in the prompt, or all new information is obviously wrong/nonsensical.
    • For the correctness judgments in "informativeness," there's no need to search on your own to verify the correctness of the presented facts. It's okay to just rely on what you know and only deduct points if things are obviously wrong.
