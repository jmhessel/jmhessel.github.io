It's just the wind.

  • The joke is funny because the husband is telling his wife that the noise she hears outside is just the wind, when in reality, it's the Earth rotating. The contrast between the innocuous explanation and the reality of the situation is what makes it funny.

  • The joke is funny because, while the husband is trying to reassure his wife that the noise she heard was just the wind, the real source of the noise is actually much more unusual: the view of Earth outside the window. The joke is funny because the husband's explanation is mundane and expected, while the real explanation is much more interesting and unexpected.

  • The joke is funny because the man's wife is clearly not convinced by his explanation for the noise she heard, which was him snoring. The joke is clever because, while the man's explanation could technically be true (it could have just been the wind), it's much more likely that the noise was him snoring, given the context.
