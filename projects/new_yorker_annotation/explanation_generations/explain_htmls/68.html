You know how it is—first you buy the giant-screen television, then nothing else seems to match.

  • The joke is funny because it's a common complaint from couples who have different taste in home décor. One person might want a giant television, while the other might want something more understated. The joke is exaggerated by the size of the appliances, which are too large to be real.

  • The joke is funny because it's a play on the phrase "you know how it is." The phrase is often used to preface a relatable story, but in this case, the story is not relatable at all. The joke is also a play on the fact that most people try to match their home furnishings, like their television, to the size of their room. But, in this case, the room was designed around the giant appliances.

  • The joke is funny because it is a common phenomenon that people buy one large item, and then find that their other belongings seem inadequate in comparison. The appliances in this room are so large that they would dwarf most people's belongings, so the joke is exaggerating the idea to make it humorous.
