I hear he forged his death certificate.

  • The joke is funny because, in order to get into heaven, one typically needs to die first. However, the candidate for angel election has presumably "died" already in order to get to heaven and is thus ineligible to run for office. The joke is clever because it playfully questions whether the candidate is actually qualified for the office he is campaigning for.

  • The joke is funny because the angel is running for office in heaven, which is absurd because angels are immortal. The joke implies that the angel is trying to cheat the system by faking his own death so that he can run for office, but because he is already dead, he can't actually forge a death certificate.

  • The joke is funny because, in order to get into heaven, one must die first. However, the candidate for angelhood has presumably cheated death somehow, and is thus not qualified to be an angel. The joke is a play on the common election tactic of mudslinging, where one candidate tries to discredit another by revealing negative information about them. In this case, the negative information is that the candidate is not actually dead.
