Ten bucks says he mentions 'sustainable growth'

  • The joke is funny because the plant is in a position of authority, yet is nonverbal and cannot actually say anything. The bet implies that the person who thinks the plant will mention sustainable growth is confident that the plant will do so because it is a popular buzzword among CEOs. The joke is clever because it is true --- many CEOs do talk about sustainable growth --- but the plant cannot actually say anything, making the bet humorous.

  • The joke is funny because the plant is a metaphor for the CEO, who is not present. The joke is that the CEO is so uninvolved in the company that he might as well be a plant, and that the only time he would be relevant is if he mentioned the buzzword "sustainable growth." The joke is also funny because it's true --- many CEOs are more concerned with growth than sustainability, and may not even know what sustainable growth means.

  • The joke is funny because the protagonist is making a bet that the plant will say something that is often said by CEOs during business meetings. The joke works because it is unlikely for a plant to be in a meeting, let alone be the CEO, so the protagonist is most likely to win the bet.
