Anything to declare?

  • The joke is funny because it's a play on words. The man is in heaven, so he's "checking in" to the afterlife. But because he's dead, he doesn't have any physical belongings with him, so he has nothing to "declare" at customs.

  • The joke is funny because it's a play on words. The man is in heaven, and so he doesn't have anything to declare --- in the sense that he doesn't have any baggage or possessions. However, he may have things to declare in the sense that he has sinned and needs to confess.

  • The joke is funny because it's a play on the phrase "anything to declare?" which is typically said to people who are crossing borders. In this context, the man has just died and gone to heaven, so he's effectively crossing a border into the afterlife. The joke is clever because it highlights the absurdity of declaring things to Saint Peter when entering heaven.
