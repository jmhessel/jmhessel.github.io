You've achieved closure.

  • The joke is funny because "closure" is a term often used in therapy to describe the process of resolving emotional wounds. However, in this context, the "closure" is literal: the patient (in the form of a casket) has died, and thus the therapist has achieved closure on the case.

  • The joke is funny because it's a play on words. The word "closure" can mean either the act of closing something (like a casket) or the act of resolving something emotionally (like a patient's trauma). In this case, the casket on the couch has been "closed" (i.e., the person inside is dead), and the patient has achieved "closure" (i.e., they have resolved their emotional issues).
