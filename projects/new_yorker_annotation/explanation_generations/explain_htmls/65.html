I have to hang up. I'm on deck.

  • The joke is funny because the speaker is on the phone, presumably with someone who is not in the room with her. The baseball players in her living room have forced her to take up a batting stance, and she tells the person on the phone that she has to hang up because she's "on deck" --- in other words, she's next up to bat. The fact that she's on the phone while this is happening adds an element of absurdity to the situation.

  • The joke is funny because of the homophones "hang up" and "on deck". In baseball, "on deck" refers to the batter who is next up to bat. However, in this context, the woman is "on deck" to be the next victim of the baseball players, who are terrorizing her living room. The fact that she has to "hang up" the phone implies that she's going to be killed.

  • The joke is funny because the person is on the phone with someone, and they say they have to hang up because they're "on deck", meaning it's their turn to bat next in the game. However, the fact that the game is taking place in the living room means that they're not actually on a baseball diamond, and hanging up the phone isn't going to help them get up to bat any faster.
