Any family history with death?

  • The joke is funny because the man in the coffin is dead, and the doctor is asking him about his family history with death. The joke plays with the idea that death is hereditary, and also with the idea that doctors are often more interested in a patient's family history than the patient themselves.

  • The joke is funny because the man in the coffin is, well, dead. He's unable to answer the doctor's question about family history with death, because he is death. The joke also plays on the common question that doctors ask about family history, which is usually relevant to the patient's health. In this case, the family history is relevant to the fact that the patient is deceased.

  • The joke is funny because it is a play on words. The doctor is asking the man if there is any history of death in his family, and the man is, well, dead. Thus, the man is the embodiment of death in his family.
