I'm only in it for the bread.

  • The joke is funny because of the play on words: "bread" can mean money/salary, but in this context, it's also a type of food that ducks eat. So, the scientist is saying that the only reason he's doing his research is for the food (bread) that the ducks will provide. This joke also pokes fun at the stereotype of scientists being in it for the money/grants rather than for the good of humanity/science.

  • The joke is funny because it is a play on the phrase "I'm only in it for the money." The speaker is a duck, which are often associated with bread (because of the popular brand of duck food, "Breaded Duck"). So, by saying "I'm only in it for the bread," the duck is saying that it is only interested in the food, not the science.
